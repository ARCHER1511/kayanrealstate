Introduction

Real estate appraisal is a field that requires expertise. It involves collecting and analyzing data related to the property being evaluated, then determining its fair value. The process is based on scientific and methodical principles, aiming to help stakeholders make informed decisions—whether for selling, buying, investing, financing, or insuring. Appraisal is one of the most important tools in the real estate market, contributing to accurate property valuation, enhancing transparency and credibility in transactions, and supporting urban planning and market regulation.

Definition of Real Estate Appraisal

It is both an art and a science used to determine property ownership rights, with specific objectives in mind while considering all factors affecting the property's value. The process includes collecting and analyzing data about the property, followed by determining its fair market value.
